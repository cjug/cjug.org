Be a Sponsor

One goal of the CJUG is to allow developers to meet, discuss and learn from our regularly scheduled presentations. These meetings are a fantastic opportunity to connect or network with recruiters and the Chicago tech industry, if one is so inclined. Benefits of this symbiotic relationship between developers and recruiters includes career opportunities, different event hosting locations, raffles, freebies and discounts. Many years ago CJUG relied on membership fees to cover a minimal set of expenses. With anticipated growth from a events for the developer community, including the ability to invite guest speakers from outside of Chicago.

If you are a Java professional you should expect to see more benefits from future CJUG activities and the quality of our presentation events. There will be “rules of engagement” between our paying sponsors and you as a member, in general we don’t want members to be overwhelmed by sponsors, so we try to make attendance to this portion of the event separate and optional.

If you are interested in becoming a sponsor we are offering multiple levels of privileges (shared or exclusive) such as speaking or presenting briefly at a meeting, emailing the user list, hosting opportunities and other advertising or marketing opportunities through CJUG. Note that this means we are closing the “free” channels of advertising such as the now disabled chicago-java-advertise@yahoogroups.com, Jobs and Promotions on the LinkedIn group, etc. Please see below for pricing and contact us through the new.cjug.org web site or email cjug@cjug.org.

Based on feedback from our members, attendees, and sponsors, they have asked us to eliminate any sponsor attendee’s that are not paid sponsors. Thus we have a policy of not allowing non-paid sponsors to attend, they will be asked to leave.

Types of sponsorship

Event Sponsor

Event sponsors are recognized for their contributions to CJUG at the event they are sponsoring. They are entitled having their name recognized during the pre meeting announcements. Sponsors that contribute both Food and Venue are entitled to give a 15 min presentation prior to the meeting.

  • Food Sponsor: Sponsor agrees to pay for food for the event.
  • Venue Sponsor: Sponsor agrees to provide or pay for the venue the event is hosted at.

"First Round’s on Me" Sponsor

Sponsor agrees to buy/subsidize drinks during or after the meeting. Sponsors are responsible for working with the establishment serving the drinks to limit expenses.

Speaker Sponsor

Sponsor agrees to pay for expenses related to bringing an out of town speaker to a CJUG meeting.
